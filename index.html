1. How does solar energy work?

Solar energy works by converting sunlight into electricity using photovoltaic (PV) panels. These panels capture sunlight and convert it into direct current (DC) electricity, which is then converted into alternating current (AC) electricity for use in your home or business.

2. What are the benefits of going solar?

Going solar offers numerous benefits, including reduced electricity bills, lower carbon footprint, increased property value, and energy independence. Solar energy is also a renewable and sustainable source of power.

3. How long do solar panels last?

Solar panels are designed to last for 25-30 years or more. Most manufacturers offer warranties that guarantee the panels will produce at least 80% of their rated output after 25 years.

4. What maintenance do solar panels require?

Solar panels require minimal maintenance. Regular cleaning to remove dirt and debris, along with periodic inspections to ensure everything is functioning properly, is usually sufficient. Most systems come with monitoring tools to help you track performance.

5. Can I go solar if I rent my home?

If you rent your home, you may still be able to benefit from solar energy through community solar programs or by discussing solar options with your landlord. These programs allow you to subscribe to a shared solar system and receive credits on your electricity bill.
